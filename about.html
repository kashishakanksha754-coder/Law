About the Firm

Independent counsel, built to last.

Verma Motwani & Co. was founded in 1994 on a simple premise: that clients are better served by a firm of independent-minded partners than by a hierarchy of billable hours.

Origin

Founded on a point of principle.

In 1994, as India opened its markets to the world, two young advocates — Arvind Verma and Ranjana Motwani — left larger firms to build a practice organised around clients’ problems rather than internal departments. The idea was unfashionable at the time: fewer practice silos, more direct partner involvement, and a refusal to take briefs that conflicted with existing relationships.

Three decades on, that idea has scaled into a firm of over 140 lawyers across three cities, without diluting the original commitment to partner-led service. Every matter at Verma Motwani & Co. is led, not merely supervised, by a partner.

Today the firm advises domestic conglomerates, multinational investors, regulators and founders on matters spanning corporate transactions, disputes, and regulatory strategy — guided by the same founding discipline: judgement first, billables second.
